IMAP

The Inventory of Maritime Archaeology in Pakistan (IMAP) is MAHIâ€™s first project, which will lay the foundation for the research and academic investigation of maritime archaeology in the country. The main aim of IMAP is to develop a heritage inventory database of all terrestrial, intertidal and submerged maritime heritage sites along the Pakistani coastline. The database will also integrate new data obtained from ongoing field-based investigations, as well as map out zones of high archaeological potential.

Phase 1 of the IMAP project began in January 2021, and has been generously supported by the Endowment Fund Trust for Preservation of the Heritage of Sindh. This is a desk-based, preparatory phase that involves analysing existing historical sources and datasets that can tell us more about the maritime archaeological resource - these can range from historical documents like travelogues, pilot books, and correspondence, to old maps, photographs and satellite imagery. More technical datasets include hydrographic, geological and environmental data, changes in which may provide insight into the evolution of cultural activity in the area. We will also be studying traditional maritime cultural practices in local fishing communities to document the intangible cultural heritage of the region.

Future phases of IMAP will involve archaeological investigations to ground-truth locations of interest identified through initial phases of the research work. Site recording techniques will include direct observational methods, remote sensing, and photographic/photogrammetric documentation.
